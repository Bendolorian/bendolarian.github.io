Overview

During the course of your apprenticeship you will build a portfolio of evidence. This portfolio of evidence such demonstrate your journey over the course of your apprenticeship. It should be rich in evidence; showcasing key elements and tasks that you do in your workplace. This portfolio will form part of Assessment method 1:An Hour long professional discussion with an independent assessor at the end of your apprenticeship In the below themes you will notice notes such as K1, B2, S3; these are the knowledge, skills and behaviours that you need to meet for you to pass the appreticeship. Assessment Method 2: Project report with questioning. At the end of your apprenticeship you will be tasks with completing a project that benefits your organisation. It is best to do soemthing that is not part of your day to day; more of an opportunity for development. Examples could be; Software deployment, system setup, hardware upgrades etc. Throughout your apprenticeship you will also be required to complete 20% of your contracted hours as Off the Job hours. College hours, meetings, training, team building, trade shows, job shadowing are all examples of off the job hours. Your off the job hours are to be recorded on Onefile with adequate reflections on each task

1. Communication

1.1 Method

Checkpoint: Does your portfolio evidence the below? And can you confidently answer these key points?
  • How do you communciate with stakeholders? (K8),(S5),(S6),(B2)
  • Does your communciation differ for urgent, medium and low level tasks?
  • How does your communication style differ when dealing with non technical people? (K8),(S5),(S6),(B2)
  • How does your communication style cater for diversity and inclusion within support tasks? (K7)
  • How do you escalate tasks? What is your role in the process? Do you follow the escalation and reflect?(S15)
